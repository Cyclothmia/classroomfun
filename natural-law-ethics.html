Principle A
Principle of Double Effect
Some acts produce both a good and a bad outcome.

This principle decides when such an act is
morally permissible.
Principle A — All 4 Must Be Met
Double Effect: 4 Conditions
1

The act itself must be good or neutral

Cannot do evil — even with a good goal in mind

2

Evil effect must NOT be directly intended

Only permitted as a regrettable side effect

3

Evil must NOT be the means to the good

"The end does NOT justify the means"

4

Good effect must outweigh the evil

More harm than good = act is not justified

Principle B
Inviolability of Life
❤️‍🔥

Life is Sacred

All human life — from conception — is created in the image and likeness of God

⚖️

All Lives Are Equal

A criminal's life = an upright person's life. Status does not determine worth.

🚫

No Innocent Life Can Be Killed

INVIOLABILITY: no innocent person can ever be directly killed

👶

Includes the Unborn

The fetus has the full natural right to life at every stage of development

Principle C
Principle of Forfeiture
🔓

An aggressor forfeits their right to life by threatening yours

🛡️

Self-Defense is Moral

Taking life in genuine self-defense is morally justified — it is not murder

⚔️

Killing ≠ Murder

Killing = taking a non-innocent life (just). Murder = taking an innocent life (unjust).

Principle D
Principle of Totality
A part exists for the good of the whole.

A diseased organ may be removed to save the body —
only when the whole body's wellbeing requires it.
🧩 Part sacrificed for the whole
🚫 Removing healthy organs = immoral
🙏 We administer, not own, our bodies
Principle E
Stewardship
🌍

God Owns All Life

We are caretakers — not owners — of our bodies and the earth

🚫

Suicide & Euthanasia = Immoral

Only God has dominion over life; it is not ours to end

💊

Duty to Seek Medical Aid

We have a moral responsibility to protect and care for the body

🌿 Also applies to Environmental Ethics — protect nature as a collective moral duty

Principle F
Just War Theory
A nation may go to defensive war against an unjust aggressor —

but only when ALL 7 conditions are strictly satisfied.

Based on Forfeiture — the aggressor forfeits their right to life

Just War — Conditions 1–4
Just War: First 4
📜

1 · Lawful Authority

Only Congress can declare war — never a private individual or group

⚖️

2 · Just Cause

Protect innocents from harm; restore rights unjustly seized

🕊️

3 · Last Resort

All diplomacy, dialogue, mediation, and negotiation must be exhausted first

💛

4 · Good Intention

Motive must be peace and justice — NOT revenge, power, or greed

Just War — Conditions 5–7
Just War: Last 3
🎯

5 · Chance of Success

Unwinnable war = immoral war — do not risk lives for a losing cause

🚫

6 · Right Means

No WMDs, no chemical or biological weapons — ordinary combat only

📏

7 · Proportional Response

Force must be proportional to the provocation — no wanton slaughter or overkill

Principle G
Justified Rebellion
Citizens may rise against a tyrannical government —

but only under rare, extreme circumstances
where ALL 5 conditions are met.
Rebellion — 5 Conditions
When Is Revolt Justified?
1

Clear, long-standing tyranny

Government has trampled fundamental rights and abandoned the common good

2

All peaceful means exhausted

Impeachment and constitutional reforms have been tried and have failed

3

Reasonable probability of success

Will it result in more good than harm? A doomed revolt is immoral.

4

Good proportionate to damage inflicted

Cost to lives and property must not outweigh what is gained

5

Decision by the majority

Must reflect the will of the people — not the opinion of a private few
